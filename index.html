AR/MR app for medical files (MRI, CT, Ultrasound)

An interactive AR/MR application for visualizing 3D models from an anatomical atlas of human organs (organs of the neck, upper torso, and head) and medical scans (MRI, CT, ultrasound), with hand-tracking controls on the Quest 3 headset.

This application was developed as part of the Hackathon "AR for Thyroid Phantom" at the Fifth Open Conference of Young Scientists of the Center for Diagnostics and Telemedicine. The presented project won second place. The application was developed by the team "Sezriv Ralizonki". Team members: Dmitriy Konstantinov, Oleg Morev, Maxim Pishchagin, Ivan Semichev.

Protocol FoxStrong Mexatron-VR (Time War)

Flight Simulator demonstrates the capabilities of mechatronic VR, conveying physical sensations of controlling a sci-fi flying vehicle with a realistic control system.
The game is cross-platform, supporting both PC and additional hardware: VR, 2DOF platform, steering wheel, pedals, gearbox and handbrake (Logitech).
The game implements unique flight physics that is transmitted to the motion platform.
The game features three enemy types: Combat space cruiser, Heavy space fighter, Light plasma tank
The game includes two large-scale locations: an asteroid belt in space and the fog-covered planet Xart with alien structures.
Available audio tutorial and two modes: Endless - timed survival; Story Mode - including 2 cut-scenes.

Protocol LiS (Mex-VR)

The project is the next version of Protocol FoxStrong - a arcade flight simulator in a Sci-fi setting, where the player controls a flying machine in space battles.
The project has been specifically redesigned to demonstrate mechatronic VR capabilities (for RTU MIREA University).
The game has undergone numerous changes - it was decided to separate the project from the previous version. For this reason, the cross-platform system and storyline were removed.
New features: Transition to arcade controls, Interactive tutorial, Improved physics and enemies, 3D radar, detailed interface, random maps (7 unique maps), plasma weapons upgrade and addition of missiles. Objective: score points by destroying enemies (cruisers, fighters, aircraft carriers) and collecting life spheres.
Equipment: PC, Quest 3, 2DOF platform, Logitech steering wheel/pedals.

Warships Defense

Warships Defense presents an interesting idea: what if in a seemingly simple "Battleship" game you had to defend instead of attack. The game is made in the arcade genre, keeping the spirit of 2000s arcade machines.

Don't waste time, you only have 30 seconds to act: move ships, repair them or remove mines. The game features endless waves of attacks, and each next wave might surprise you. Has a Like in leaderboard similar to the arcade machines.

The project is adapted for WebGL.
